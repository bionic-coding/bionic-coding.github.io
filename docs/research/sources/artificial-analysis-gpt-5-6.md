# GPT-5.6 benchmarks across Intelligence, Speed and Cost

_Artificial Analysis — independent benchmark house. They "supported OpenAI with pre-release evaluation of GPT-5.6 Sol, Terra, and Luna." Numbers are on the AA Intelligence Index **v4.1**; "max" = max reasoning effort. Benchmarks move — date every figure._

## Headline (verbatim)

> GPT-5.6 Sol comes close second to Claude Fable 5 in the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index at one third of the cost, and leads the Artificial Analysis Coding Agent Index in OpenAI's Codex harness.

## Numbers

- **Intelligence Index (max):** Sol **59** (1 pt below Fable 5, at ~⅓ the cost — $1.04/task), Terra **55**, Luna **51**. Sol uses fewer output tokens (~15k/task vs GPT-5.5's 16k) and is "more intelligent than Claude Opus 4.8 (max), GLM-5.2 (max), and Gemini 3.5 Flash."
- **Coding Agent Index (max, in Codex):** Sol **leads at 80**, topping all three evals (DeepSWE, Terminal-Bench v2, SWE-Atlas-QnA); Terra 77, Luna 75. Sol's per-task cost is ~40% cheaper than Fable 5 (max) and ~10% cheaper than Opus 4.8 (max) in Claude Code.
- **AA-Briefcase (knowledge work):** Sol (max) ranks **second only to Fable 5 (max)**, but has the **highest Presentation Elo of any model**. Fable 5 still leads overall, largely on Rubric Score (56% vs Sol's 42%) and Analytical Quality Elo (1764 vs Sol's 1592).
- **Pricing (independent confirmation):** Sol **$5 / $30**, Terra **$2.50 / $15**, Luna **$1 / $6** per MTok. First OpenAI models with **cache-write pricing** (1.25× input); 90% cache-read discount retained; introduces a **max reasoning-effort** level (matching Anthropic).

## The takeaway (for the "benchmarks mislead" point)

AA's own picture is split by task: **Sol leads coding** (Coding Agent Index) and presentation polish, while **Fable 5 leads overall intelligence** (barely) and analytical rubric quality in AA-Briefcase. Different suites crown different winners — this AA framing gives Sol the coding edge, whereas SWE-Bench Pro (a different suite) puts Fable ahead. No single "best."
